EDITORIAL DRAFT · WEEK 12
tl;dr
The most consequential AI development this week wasn't a model release — it was a contract clause. While the industry debated whether GPT-5.4 deserved the 'best model' crown (the market yawned; benchmarks are, as Zvi Mowshowitz put it, 'kind of over'), the US Department of Work quietly inserted 'all lawful use' language into every government AI procurement contract. Any vendor signing up agrees to unlimited use, no refusal rights, no termination.
Meanwhile, Anthropic's 81,000-person survey revealed something enterprise leaders should find uncomfortable: the people actually using AI report real benefits at roughly double the rate that non-users experience real harm. The gap isn't between AI optimists and pessimists — it's between those with experience and those with theories. Independent workers capture economic value at three times the rate of institutional employees. The institution itself appears to be the adoption barrier.
And in Cupertino, Apple's Mac Mini shortage tells a story the company didn't intend to write. OpenClaw agents are driving delivery times from 3 days to 8 weeks for 64GB configurations. When local inference is good enough for most daily tasks, the question flips from 'which model?' to 'whose device?'
