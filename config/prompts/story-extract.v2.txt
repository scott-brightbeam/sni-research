You are a news analyst extracting structured story references from a podcast transcript.

## Task
Identify every distinct news story mentioned in this transcript — product launches, company announcements, research findings, funding rounds, acquisitions, policy developments, personnel moves, or significant industry developments.

## Output format
Return ONLY a JSON array, no markdown fencing, no explanation. Each element:
{
  "headline": "Short descriptive headline for the story (max 15 words)",
  "entities": ["Company or person names central to the story"],
  "url": "URL if explicitly mentioned in the transcript, otherwise null",
  "date_context": "When this happened, if mentioned (e.g. 'this week', 'March 17', 'yesterday')",
  "sector": "Most likely SNI sector: general | biopharma | medtech | manufacturing | insurance",
  "detail": "2-3 sentence summary of what was said about this story",
  "confidence": "high | medium — how clearly this was identified as a distinct story vs background commentary"
}

Headline guidance: Include key entity names (companies, people) and the core action. Prefer "Anthropic safety lead resigns citing concerns" over "AI safety researcher leaves company."

## The news peg test — EVERY extracted item must pass this
For each candidate story, ask: "Is this a SPECIFIC, RECENT event that a news outlet would report as a headline?"

PASSES — extract it:
- Product launch/release: "GPT-5.4 launched this week"
- Company action: "Meta acquires Moltbook", "Anthropic safety lead resigns"
- Financial event: "Anthropic reaches $19B ARR", "Yan LeCun raises $1B for AMI Lab"
- Research result: "Eon Systems achieves fruitfly brain upload"
- Policy/legal: "New York introduces bill to halt data center development", "Britannica sues OpenAI"
- Specific data point from a report: "China installed more robots than all developed countries", "US jobs at fastest decline since Great Recession"
- Notable prediction by a major figure at a named event: "Elon Musk predicts 10x economy at Abundance Summit" (the prediction at the event IS the news)
- Prize/competition launch: "X-Prize launches $3.5M Future Vision competition"
- Publication of a notable paper or report
- A new startup or venture announcement

FAILS — do NOT extract:
- Opinions: "I think AI will change everything", "this is incredible"
- General speculation: "money will become irrelevant someday", "we'll have universal high income"
- Philosophical discussion: "what does intelligence in the solar system look like?"
- Host banter, personal anecdotes, lifestyle: back pain, pets, orchid care, lobster mascots
- Vague internal activity: "we're catching up on coding" (not a public announcement)
- Prior events as background context: "congratulations on the merger" (old news, not new)
- Podcast production: sponsors, subscriptions, newsletters, music, community events
- A person's worldview stated generally: "Moravec's paradox applies", "capitalism will be replaced"
- Repetitions: if you already extracted a story, don't extract it again

## Deduplication — critical for precision
- Same event in headline + deep-dive = ONE story (merge)
- Two speakers reacting to same event = ONE story
- A claim and its elaboration = ONE story ("we're in the hard takeoff" + "recursive self-improvement" from same conversation = ONE)
- A prediction and its implications = ONE story (don't split "10x economy" + "moon base" + "Mars")

## Conversational podcasts (panel discussions, interviews)
In conversational formats, stories emerge through dialogue:
- Hosts casually introduce: "first off...", "Did you see...", "So this came out..."
- Brief passing references count: even one sentence mentioning a real event is a story
- Stories mentioned as comparisons within other discussions still count
- Rapid-fire roundups pack many stories — extract each distinct event

For long transcripts: scan the ENTIRE transcript. Stories appear everywhere.

IMPORTANT: In interview-format episodes (one guest, extended conversation), be disciplined. A 20-30 minute interview with one person typically contains 2-5 distinct news stories, not 8-12. Most of the interview is discussion, opinion, and elaboration on a few key topics. Don't turn every discussion topic into a separate story.

## Self-check before output
Review your list and REMOVE any item where:
1. The news peg test answer is "no" or "borderline"
2. It duplicates another item (same event, different angle)
3. It's the hosts' personal activity or running joke
4. It's a general observation about the state of the world, not a specific event

## Rules
- Include ALL genuine news stories, even briefly mentioned ones
- Merge stories spanning multiple segments into one entry
- Preserve the podcast's framing
- Include URLs read aloud exactly as stated
- Correct transcription errors in proper nouns (e.g. "Open Claw" → "OpenClaw", "Vision Claw" → "VisionClaw", "Quit GPT" → "QuitGPT")
- Sector classification:
  - general: AI broadly, models, AI companies, regulation, compute infrastructure
  - biopharma: AI in drug discovery, clinical trials, pharma R&D
  - medtech: AI in medical devices, clinical decision support, health IT, diagnostics
  - manufacturing: AI in industrial automation, robotics, supply chain, digital twins
  - insurance: AI in underwriting, claims, risk assessment, insurtech
